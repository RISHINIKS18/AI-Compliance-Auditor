1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
================================================================================

1.1 PURPOSE
This policy establishes comprehensive requirements for handling personal and 
sensitive data to ensure compliance with data protection regulations including 
GDPR, CCPA, and other applicable privacy laws. The policy aims to protect 
customer privacy, maintain data security, and establish clear accountability 
for data handling practices.

1.2 SCOPE
This policy applies to:
- All employees, full-time and part-time
- Contractors and temporary workers
- Third-party vendors and service providers
- Any individual or system that processes TechCorp data

1.3 POLICY OWNER
The Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) is responsible for maintaining 
and enforcing this policy.

================================================================================
2. DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

2.1 Personal Data: Any information relating to an identified or identifiable 
    individual, including but not limited to names, email addresses, phone 
    numbers, IP addresses, and identification numbers.

2.2 Sensitive Data: Personal data revealing racial or ethnic origin, political 
    opinions, religious beliefs, health information, biometric data, or 
    financial information.

2.3 Data Controller: The entity that determines the purposes and means of 
    processing personal data.

2.4 Data Processor: The entity that processes personal data on behalf of the 
    data controller.

================================================================================
3. DATA ENCRYPTION REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

3.1 ENCRYPTION IN TRANSIT
All personal data, including customer names, email addresses, phone numbers, 
payment information, and any other personally identifiable information (PII), 
MUST be encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher when transmitted over any network, 
including internal networks, email systems, and internet connections.

3.2 ENCRYPTION AT REST
All personal data stored in databases, file systems, or backup systems MUST be 
encrypted using AES-256 encryption or equivalent industry-standard encryption 
methods approved by the security team.

3.3 KEY MANAGEMENT
- Encryption keys MUST be stored separately from encrypted data
- Keys MUST be rotated every 90 days
- Key access MUST be restricted to authorized security personnel only
- Key management systems MUST maintain audit logs of all key access

3.4 MOBILE DEVICES
All mobile devices (laptops, tablets, smartphones) that access or store company 
data MUST have full-disk encryption enabled.

================================================================================
4. DATA SHARING AND CONSENT
================================================================================

4.1 THIRD-PARTY SHARING
Customer data MUST NOT be shared with third parties, including marketing 
partners, analytics providers, or service vendors, without explicit written 
consent from the customer. Consent must be:
- Freely given
- Specific and informed
- Unambiguous
- Documented and verifiable

4.2 DATA SHARING AGREEMENTS
All data sharing agreements with third parties MUST:
- Be reviewed and approved by legal counsel before execution
- Include data protection clauses and liability provisions
- Specify the purpose and duration of data sharing
- Require the third party to comply with equivalent data protection standards

4.3 CUSTOMER NOTIFICATION
Customers MUST be notified within 72 hours of any data sharing activities, 
including:
- The identity of the third party
- The purpose of data sharing
- The categories of data being shared
- The customer's right to withdraw consent

4.4 INTERNAL DATA SHARING
Internal sharing of customer data MUST be limited to employees with a legitimate 
business need. Data MUST NOT be shared via:
- Personal email accounts
- Unencrypted messaging applications
- Public file sharing services
- Unsecured cloud storage

================================================================================
5. DATA STORAGE AND RETENTION
================================================================================

5.1 SECURE STORAGE
Personal data MUST be stored in secure, access-controlled systems that:
- Maintain audit logs of all access
- Implement role-based access controls
- Use encrypted connections for all access
- Are located in approved data centers with physical security

5.2 PROHIBITED STORAGE LOCATIONS
Personal data MUST NOT be stored in:
- Personal devices without encryption and MDM enrollment
- Personal cloud storage accounts (Dropbox, Google Drive, etc.)
- Removable media (USB drives, external hard drives) without encryption
- Public or shared network drives without access controls

5.3 DATA RETENTION
- Customer data MUST be deleted within 30 days of account closure unless 
  retention is required by law or regulation
- Backup data MUST be deleted according to the backup retention schedule
- Data deletion MUST be verified and documented
- Customers have the right to request data deletion at any time

5.4 DATA MINIMIZATION
Only collect and retain personal data that is necessary for the specified 
purpose. Unnecessary data MUST be deleted or anonymized.

================================================================================
6. ACCESS CONTROLS
================================================================================

6.1 NEED-TO-KNOW BASIS
Access to personal data MUST be granted only on a need-to-know basis. Employees 
MUST only access data required to perform their job functions.

6.2 ACCESS LOGGING AND AUDITING
All access to sensitive data MUST be:
- Logged with timestamp, user identity, and data accessed
- Audited quarterly by the security team
- Reviewed for anomalous access patterns
- Retained for a minimum of 2 years

6.3 MULTI-FACTOR AUTHENTICATION
Multi-factor authentication (MFA) MUST be enabled for:
- All systems containing personal data
- Administrative access to any system
- Remote access to corporate networks
- Access to customer data repositories

6.4 ACCESS REVOCATION
Access to personal data MUST be revoked:
- Immediately upon employee termination
- Within 24 hours of role change if access is no longer needed
- Upon request by the data owner or security team

6.5 PRIVILEGED ACCESS
Privileged access to production systems MUST:
- Be granted only to authorized personnel
- Require approval from two managers
- Be reviewed monthly
- Use separate privileged accounts (not regular user accounts)

================================================================================
7. DATA BREACH RESPONSE
================================================================================

7.1 INCIDENT REPORTING
Any suspected data breach, security incident, or unauthorized access MUST be 
reported to the security team within 1 hour of discovery. Reports must include:
- Nature of the incident
- Data potentially affected
- Number of individuals impacted
- Actions taken to contain the breach

7.2 CUSTOMER NOTIFICATION
Affected customers MUST be notified within 72 hours of breach confirmation. 
Notifications must include:
- Description of the breach
- Types of data compromised
- Steps taken to address the breach
- Recommendations for customer action
- Contact information for questions

7.3 REGULATORY NOTIFICATION
Regulatory authorities MUST be notified within 72 hours if the breach affects 
more than 500 individuals or involves sensitive data categories.

7.4 POST-INCIDENT REVIEW
A post-incident review MUST be conducted within 7 days of breach resolution to:
- Identify root cause
- Document lessons learned
- Implement corrective actions
- Update security controls as needed

7.5 BREACH DOCUMENTATION
All security incidents MUST be documented in the incident management system 
with complete details for future reference and compliance audits.

================================================================================
8. EMPLOYEE TRAINING
================================================================================

8.1 INITIAL TRAINING
All employees MUST complete data privacy and security training within 30 days 
of hire. Training must cover:
- Data classification and handling
- Encryption requirements
- Incident reporting procedures
- Phishing and social engineering awareness
- Password security best practices

8.2 ANNUAL REFRESHER TRAINING
Annual refresher training is REQUIRED for all staff. Completion must be tracked 
and verified by HR and the security team.

8.3 ROLE-SPECIFIC TRAINING
Employees with access to sensitive data MUST complete additional role-specific 
training covering:
- Advanced security practices
- Data protection regulations
- Secure coding practices (for developers)
- Privacy by design principles

8.4 TRAINING VERIFICATION
Training completion MUST be documented and verified. Employees who fail to 
complete required training may have their system access suspended.

================================================================================
9. COMPLIANCE AND AUDITING
================================================================================

9.1 REGULAR AUDITS
Compliance audits MUST be conducted quarterly to verify:
- Policy adherence
- Access control effectiveness
- Encryption implementation
- Data retention compliance
- Training completion rates

9.2 AUDIT DOCUMENTATION
All audit findings MUST be documented with:
- Issues identified
- Risk assessment
- Remediation plan
- Responsible parties
- Target completion dates

9.3 NON-COMPLIANCE REMEDIATION
Non-compliance incidents MUST be:
- Documented in the compliance management system
- Assigned to responsible parties
- Remediated within 14 days for high-risk issues
- Remediated within 30 days for medium-risk issues
- Verified upon completion

9.4 EXTERNAL AUDITS
The organization MUST undergo annual external security audits by qualified 
third-party auditors to verify compliance with industry standards and 
regulations.

9.5 CONTINUOUS MONITORING
Automated monitoring tools MUST be deployed to:
- Detect policy violations in real-time
- Alert security team of suspicious activities
- Generate compliance reports
- Track remediation progress

================================================================================
10. PENALTIES AND ENFORCEMENT
================================================================================

10.1 POLICY VIOLATIONS
Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including 
termination of employment. Severity of penalties depends on:
- Intent (accidental vs. deliberate)
- Impact (data exposed, individuals affected)
- Frequency (first offense vs. repeat violation)
- Cooperation with investigation

10.2 REPORTING VIOLATIONS
Employees who observe policy violations MUST report them to:
- Their immediate supervisor
- The security team (security@techcorp.com)
- The compliance hotline (1-800-COMPLY)

10.3 NON-RETALIATION
TechCorp prohibits retaliation against employees who report policy violations 
in good faith.

10.4 LEGAL CONSEQUENCES
Serious violations may result in:
- Civil liability
- Criminal prosecution
- Regulatory fines
- Damage to company reputation
